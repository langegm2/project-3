METHANE EXPLAINED

One of the largest contributers to a warming climate is Methane gas. Not only is it a significant contributer to climate change, but it also contributes to poor air quality. Sources of methane emissions include agriculture, energy, waste and others.

GLOBAL ANALYSIS

This analysis uses a global data set of methane emissions to give perspective as to what regions of the world are contributing the most, and also identifies the sources of methane emissions by region across the globe.

ENERGY RELATED METHANE EMISSIONS

A byproduct of gathering energy sources like natural gas, oil, and others can be methane gas. There are various ways this gas can be released or even saved for other use. Three options to release methane as a byproduct of energy harvesting are flaring, venting and fugitive. Venting is considered the worst as it involves a simple release to the atmosphere. Flaring involves burning the gas and reduces emissions but contribues to pollution in other ways. Fugitive is used to describe unwanted and uncontrolled release of methane emissions.
